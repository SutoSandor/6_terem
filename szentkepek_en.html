Sacred Pictures


Small, graphic, religious pictures belonging to the scope of personal devotion. Their subjects include biblical scenes, Jesus Christ, the Virgin Mary, the saints and the truths of faith. With the appearance of graphic reproduction processes (wood and copper engraving, etching) they became mass-produced, they were purchased at indulgence events. The sacred pictures were stored between the pages of prayer books, hung on walls, fixed on bedboards, pasted in cabinets, crates, pilgrims pinned them on their hats. From the middle of the 19th century masses of lithographs and then rudimentary colour prints replaced the images engraved on copper common for centuries. The Bouasse-Lebel holy cards from the Parisian Turgis publishing company and the coloured steel engraved pictures and stone prints imported from Benziger company in Einsiedeln with their Biedermeier moods and paper lace decorations catered to the needs of a genteel, civic taste.
