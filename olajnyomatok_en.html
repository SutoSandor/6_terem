Oil prints with religious themes


Paper-based, plane-like representation prepared in multiple copies. Coloured oil prints became wide-spread in the second half of the 19th century, typically in peasant or small town interiors. In our country oil prints began to be imported in large numbers from Vienna in the 1860s, and then they were made in Hungary also in the 1880s. Contrary to the more costly individual works of art, their popularity was due to their feature of catering for the needs of the masses. In the homes the prints were primarily placed in the holy corner. They were arranged according to the rules of symmetry, often with ornament plates, coloured holy candles or other small artefacts of worship.
